What is HackSoc?

HackSoc is the computer science society here at the University of York, although you don't need to be a computer science student to participate. We run a mixture of academic and social events, providing a way for people to learn about things they're interested in, which may not necessarily be covered by the actual course.

Whilst plans for the next academic year are a bit hazy at the moment, we will be running an intermediate-level Python course in the Spring term, and hope to run some introductory courses in the Autumn term.

You can find more information on our website, hacksoc.org.

What events do you run?

We run a mixture of academic and social events. Our academic events consist of lectures and seminars, which anyone can ask to run. We try to run one or two courses each term as well. Our staple social events are boardgames evenings and visiting pubs, although we're considering branching out.

We're also looking into the possibility of guest lectures and trips for next year, although currently these are very provisional.

How can I get in touch?

You can email the committee directly at hack@yusu.org, subscribe to our mailing list, or join us on IRC in #cs-york on freenode. If you've never used IRC or a mailing list before don't worry, you will soon pick it up.

What do I do on moving in day?

Firstly, make sure you know which car park is closest to your college, as carrying your stuff all over the place is not fun. There are lots of car parks, so you should be able to find one fairly close to your new home. Once you arrive, there should be a plethora of helpers wandering around, guiding people to and fro, and helping people transport their things. You'll want to go get your keys and university card, and someone will probably grab you and take you to where that's happening as soon as you get out of the car.

Once you have your keys, go find your room and proceed to move things over there. Again, there will be people to help you if you get lost, so don't be afraid to ask. Furthermore, there are trolleys (Â£10 deposit required), so if you're lucky you might be able to get one before they are all gone.

Leave your door open, and go introuce yourself to your flatmates. Remember: they're just as confused as you are. Later in the day, your STYCs (Second and Third Year Contacts) will arrive, these are two people who are assigned to look after your flat for freshers' week, and are typically very nice people. Their job is to answer any questions you might have, so ask away.

To finish the day, there will be some sort of college welcome event, which your STYCs will take you to.

Who will look after me?

Fortunately, the university and YUSU are rather used to people being lost when they arrive, and so provide many points of contact if you need help.

During freshers week you will have two STYCs (Second and Third Year Contacts), whose job is to provide any help you need during that week. You will also be assigned a supervisor, an academic in your department who will look after you for all of your time at York. Finally, your college will have a welfare team, YUSU has a welfare team, and there are other societies like Nightline who also provide support.

We all know that starting a totally new life can be scary and confusing, so if you need any help at all, don't be afraid to ask anyone. Even if they can't solve your problem, they should be able to point you to someone who can.

What are the colleges?

For organisational purposes, the university is divided into a number of undergraduate colleges, and one postgraduate college (Wentworth). However, unlike many universities, which college you end up in isn't terribly important. You will be a member of a college for your time at York, and the major thing this affects is where you live in your first year. Colleges do run events such as film nights, trips, toasties, and so on, but in general, anyone from any college can turn up to any event.

How do I apply for accommodation, and where should I live?

Once you have accepted an unconditional offer (that is, you have your results and confirmed that York is where you'll be going), you will receive an email giving you access to the online application system for acommodation. On results day, the emails are sent out over a 48 hour period randomly, so you may not get yours until the next day, but don't worry. Once you make your application, you'll receive a room offer within three days, after which you must pay a deposit to confirm it. If you don't confirm within a few days, your offer will be withdrawn, and you'll have to apply again.

Which college you apply to depends mostly on what you'll be studying, and what you want. Some colleges offer en-suite rooms, some offer catering, some are on the main campus, some are on the Heslington East campus. Hes East houses Goodricke and Langwith colleges, and also the departments of Computer Science; Theatre, Film, and Television; and Law and Management.
